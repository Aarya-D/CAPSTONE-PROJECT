"Poor" in turn is defined as "having insufficient wealth to meet the necessities or comforts of life or to live in a manner considered acceptable in a society."2 Central to these definitions are "needs," "comforts," and living "in a manner considered acceptable in a society." Taken together, they characterize poverty as an economic concept that refers to limited resources: being in poverty indicates a level of hardship or deprivation because resources are insufficient to meet a level of material well-being.
